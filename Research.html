Research Areas

ASCII Lab conducts highly interdisciplinary research focusing on intelligent mechatronics, advanced signal processing, and autonomous cyber-physical systems to solve critical real-world challenges.

Home / Research

Machine Learning in Biological Sensors

Leveraging advanced machine learning architectures (such as LDA-SVM and KNN classifiers) to interpret complex bio-impedance spectroscopy data. Our research focuses on developing disposable, highly sensitive diagnostic tools for rapid bioexcretion sensing and fluid analysis.

Key Focus Areas
Biological Sensors Machine Learning Impedimetry Analysis Point-of-Care (PoC) Devices

UAV Systems & Underwater Drones

Pioneering robust Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC) algorithms for autonomous platforms. We develop intelligent multi-agent consensus frameworks for Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) and specialized localization paradigms for Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs) operating in highly degraded subsea environments.

Key Focus Areas
Guidance, Navigation & Control UAV Systems Underwater Drones (ROV/AUV) Swarm Intelligence
View Related Projects

Precision Agriculture

Deploying state-of-the-art Deep Learning models and Digital Image Processing techniques to optimize agricultural yields. Our work encompasses non-destructive ripeness detection using computer vision, automated assessment of plant pathology, and UAV-assisted crop monitoring.

Key Focus Areas
Image Processing Deep Learning Crop Health Monitoring Non-Destructive Testing
Explore Technologies

Sensor Electronics & Signal Processing

Designing sophisticated fractional-order filters and processing architectures to extract high-accuracy diagnostic features from physiological signals (ECG, EEG, EMG). This domain also extends into the mechanical design and real-time kinematic control systems for assistive Exoskeletons.

Key Focus Areas
ECG, EEG, EMG Analysis Exoskeleton Design Fractional Order Systems Sensor Electronics
Read Publications

Interdisciplinary Data Analysis

Synthesizing and evaluating complex datasets across disparate engineering fields. Whether parsing underwater robotic telemetry for structural health monitoring or executing predictive modeling for formability in micro-incremental sheet forming (μ-SPIF) processes, we specialize in high-level analytical abstraction.

Key Focus Areas
Underwater Robotics Data Incremental Forming Models Predictive Analytics Cyber Physical Systems
